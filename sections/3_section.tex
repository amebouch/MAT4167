\subsection{Monogenic Field}
\begin{flushleft}
    \quad\textbf{Definition:} Let K be an algebraic number field. K is monogenic if there exists a $\theta$ 
    in $\mathcal{O}_K$ such that 
    \[
    \mathcal{O}_K = \mathbb{Z} + \mathbb{Z}\theta + \cdots + \mathbb{Z}\theta^{n-1}
    \]
    with $[K:\mathbb{Q}] = n$ (the degree of $K$ over $\mathbb{Q}$).
\end{flushleft}
\bigskip
We are interested in factorizing ideals of a monogenic number field into a product of 
prime ideals. A theorem proved by Dedekind in 1878 provides us with such a method, by considering congruences between the prime Factorization of 
an element of K and a product of irreducible polynomials in $\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}$.
